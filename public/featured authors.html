Featured Authors

Arundhati Roy

Arundhati Roy is an Indian writer, best known for her book The God of Small Things (1997), which won the Man Booker Prize for Fiction in 1997.In addition to her fiction, Roy has written extensively in the form of essays and non-fiction works that address a wide range of political and social issues, including globalization, environmentalism, and human rights.

Stephen Coonts

Stephen Coonts is the author of sixteen New York Times bestselling books. A former naval aviator and Vietnam combat veteran. Coonts is known for his meticulous research and attention to detail, which lends authenticity to his stories. His works often combine elements of suspense, action, and political intrigue, appealing to fans of military and adventure fiction.

Stephen King

Stephen Edwin King is a prolific American author known for his works of horror, supernatural fiction, suspense, and fantasy.King has published several works under the pseudonym Richard Bachman, including "Rage" and "The Running Man," to explore darker themes and avoid market saturation.

John Green

John Green is the author of Looking for Alaska, The Fault in Our Stars, and other popular novels.Green's work has significantly influenced the young adult genre, often credited with ushering in a new era of realistic and relatable teen fiction.

Paulo Coelho

Paulo Coelho is known for his novel The Alchemist and has sold over 100 million books worldwide.Coelho’s writing often incorporates elements of mysticism and spirituality, drawing from various philosophical and religious traditions.His prose is characterized by simplicity and clarity, making profound concepts accessible to a wide audience.

Robin Sharma

Robin Sharma is a Canadian author best known for The Monk Who Sold His Ferrari series and his work on leadership and motivation.He is a popular speaker at conferences and events, sharing his insights on personal development and leadership with audiences worldwide.

R.K. Narayan

R.K. Narayan was a celebrated Indian author known for his works set in the fictional South Indian town of Malgudi.R.K. Narayan's ability to weave relatable narratives with humor and compassion has made him a beloved figure in Indian literature, and his fictional town of Malgudi remains a significant cultural reference.

Amitav Ghosh

Amitav Ghosh is an acclaimed Indian writer known for his historical and environmental fiction.He often blends fiction with historical events, exploring the interconnectedness of cultures and the impact of colonialism.His contributions to literature have established him as a pivotal figure in contemporary Indian writing.

Arthur Conan Doyle

Arthur Conan Doyle is the creator of Sherlock Holmes, the legendary fictional detective.He also wrote plays, poetry, and non-fiction, including works on spiritualism, a subject he became passionate about later in life.Doyle was an avid supporter of spiritualism and wrote extensively on the subject, believing in the possibility of communication with the dead.

Dr. Seuss

Theodor Seuss Geisel, known as Dr. Seuss, was a renowned author of children's books, including The Cat in the Hat.Dr. Seuss's stories have been adapted into animated films, stage productions, and television specials, further solidifying his influence on popular culture.

Sidney Sheldon

Sidney Sheldon was a bestselling author known for his novels of suspense and drama.His novels have been adapted into television miniseries and films, further solidifying his place in popular culture.His novels frequently feature themes of power, betrayal, and the complexities of human relationships.His novels often featured strong female protagonists and suspenseful plots, appealing primarily to a female audience.
